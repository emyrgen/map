How MAP Works

A magnetic field turns abrasive media into an elastic polishing brush.

This mechanism makes the platform understandable. Magnetic fields organize ferroabrasive media into a flexible finishing zone. The process conditions the surface while reducing reliance on rigid tool contact and slurry-heavy chemistry.

Mechanism visual. The magnetic field forms a controlled abrasive brush over the work surface, giving the visitor a clear mental picture of how MAP works.

Why the mechanism sells

Controlled field. Adaptive abrasive media. Cleaner precision finishing.

Elastic brush actionThe field-shaped abrasive zone conforms to precision surfaces instead of behaving like a conventional rigid polishing tool.
Surface-layer improvementThe pulsing magnetic field is presented as improving the structure of the surface layer during finishing.
More than polishingMAP also supports surface formation, cleansing, preparation before coating, preparation before welding, and finishing for adhesion or diffusion-driven processes.
